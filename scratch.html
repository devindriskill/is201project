What is improv?

Improvisational Comedy is a way to be creative and have fun. It involves acting out scenes and songs with no script or plan prepared before hand. Everything is made up on the spot, with actors saying the first thing that comes into their head. To help both guide their creativity and add some challenge, improvisors get suggestions from the audience of what to do a scene about. With lots of pracitce, good improv comedians are able to work together to create charactors, conflics, and stories on the fly that lead to some pretty wacky scenarios. The rules of Improv

There are a few rules, or rather best practices of improv that actors should abide by if they want to make a good, funny scene. They are as follows
  • Always say "Yes, and..." This means that if your scene partner makes a character or plot choice, you don't deny it. You always go with it and build on it
  • Don't try to be funny. If you are trying to be the funniest guy on stage, you will likely act cringy and actually become the least funny guy on stage. The funniest improvisors just try to make a good, realistic story
  • Create the best scenes possible by establishing the 3 basic elements of a scene. All improv scenes should have:
    1. a relationship between the characters
    2. a location
    3. an internal or external conflict
